Why this exists

Oakridge is more than a school

Founded 45 years ago on a simple belief — that every child has strengths worth nurturing — Oakridge was built on character, faith, and the conviction that a school should be open to everyone who shares those values. That founding spirit is as relevant today as it was with the first nine students.

It's the kind of school families come back to. Alumni send their own children here. Teachers stay for decades. The Six Pillars aren't a poster on the wall — they leave a mark that lasts a lifetime.

Like many independent schools, Oakridge reached a critical crossroads — enrollment pressure and the absence of a strong endowment made long-term stability uncertain. Tuition alone has limits. The community came together at exactly the right moment.

Now the work continues. Families and alumni are working alongside school leadership to build the structure this school deserves — a strategy, an endowment, and a governance foundation that can carry Oakridge into its next chapter.

This isn't about a single donation or a single event. It's about everyone who has ever called Oakridge home deciding together that it's worth protecting — and worth building on.
